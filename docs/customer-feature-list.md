# Treehouse Finds — A digital tool for finding real treasure

> **Live at:** app.kentuckytreehouse.com
> **For:** Kentucky shoppers, antique vendors, and the malls that host them

---

## The idea

Treehouse Finds isn't a marketplace. There's no checkout. No urgency. No infinite scroll engineered to keep you on your phone.

It's a quieter thing — a digital tool that helps you find treasure in real places. Vendors photograph what's on their shelves. Shoppers scout the landscape from the couch, save what catches their eye, then drive over to see it in person. Every digital affordance points back to a real booth in a real antique mall in Kentucky.

**Find treasure. In real places. With your real Saturday.**

---

## For Shoppers

### Scout the landscape

Open the app. The Home feed shows finds from active malls across Kentucky — every photograph is something a vendor has on a shelf right now, in a booth you can walk into this weekend.

You can stay broad ("show me everything across Kentucky") or scope to a specific mall ("just America's Antique Mall today"). The picker travels with you across the app.

### Search by what you actually want

Type "brass" or "tobacco basket" or "mid-century" into the glass search bar at the top of the feed. Behind the scenes, every find has been catalogued by an AI vision model that reads each photograph and writes down what's actually in it — material, era, motif, kind of object — so a search for "brass candlestick" finds the brass candlestick even if the vendor just titled it "$45 OBO."

### See the place on a map

Tap **Map** in the navigation. Kentucky shows up in a warm-cream cartographic style with a leaf pin at every active treehouse location. Tap a pin and a small postcard tells you what's there: how many booths, how many finds, the actual street address. Tap **Explore** and the feed scopes to that mall.

### See how far it is from where you are right now

On every find detail page, on every booth page, on every map callout — a small italic line tells you how far away the place is. "2.7 mi" or "23 mi" or "Across town." The number updates as you move.

When you're ready to go, tap **Take the Trip** and Apple Maps or Google Maps opens with the route already plotted. The bridge from your couch to the booth is one tap.

### Save what catches your eye

Tap the heart on any find — it's saved. Tap the bookmark on any booth — you'll see new finds from that curator as they post.

Your saves don't disappear when the find sells. They stay in your Saved tab as a record of "I saw this once and it spoke to me." Saved finds are grouped by mall so you can plan an outing around a half-dozen things in one place instead of chasing one find across three counties.

### Sign in once and take everything with you

Type your email. We send you a magic link. Tap it. You're in.

No password. No social-login carousel. No account-creation form with eighteen fields. Once you've signed in, your saves, bookmarks, and handle travel with you across every device — phone, iPad, web — and your scouting history is yours, not the app's.

### Share what you find

Found a booth a friend would love? A specific find your mom would want for her kitchen? Every find, every booth, and every mall has a paper-airplane share affordance. Send a text. Share a QR code. Copy the link. Booths can also be emailed with a rich card that recipients can save or print.

### Mark the moment you found it in person

When you drive to a saved find and see it on the shelf in real life, a small ✓ Found checkmark lets you mark the crossing — the digital saw it became the physical found it. The save was an invitation. The check is the visit.

## For Vendors

### A permanent digital storefront

Every vendor gets a `/shelf/[your-booth]` page — a clean, photographic catalog of everything you have on your real shelf, presented as a polaroid-style grid with your booth identity at the top.

It's not a shopping cart. It's a window display that lives on the internet — shoppers see what you have, save what they want, and come find it in your booth.

### A real curator identity

Recent updates let you give your booth a face: a circular avatar, a short bio in italic serif type, links to your Facebook and Instagram so shoppers can follow you between visits, and custom directions for finding your specific booth inside the mall ("Back left corner, near the vintage glassware aisle").

When a shopper opens your booth page, they don't see a row in a database — they see who curates this real place.

### Add a find in three taps

Open the app. Tap **Add a Find**. Photograph the item. Optionally photograph the inventory tag pinned to it. Tap publish.

That's the whole flow. Behind the scenes, our vision model reads the printed tag (or the photograph itself), pulls out the title and price, writes a story-style caption in our voice, and tags the find for search — all invisibly, before you've finished walking back to your sticker gun.

You can edit any of it. Title, price, status, caption. Nothing publishes silently.

### Sell from your real booth, not a checkout cart

Treehouse Finds doesn't process payment. We don't take a percentage of your sale. The job of the app is to bring the shopper to your booth — what happens at the register is between you and them.

When something sells, mark it sold with a tap. Saved finds get a "saved-but-sold" treatment so the shopper knows the story.

### Manage multiple booths from one account

Vendors with booths in more than one mall — or more than one booth in a single mall — switch between them with a booth picker. One sign-in, all your inventory.

### Share your booth like a business card

A built-in **Share Booth** affordance generates SMS-ready links, QR codes you can print and post in your real-world booth, and rich emails for outreach to followers. The QR code is the bridge from the printed paper sign in the booth back to the digital storefront.

### Sign in passwordlessly

A 6-digit code in your email gets you into your dashboard. No password to forget. No security questions. The app remembers you across devices once you've signed in once.

## How it feels

Slow. Warm. Photographic. Made of paper and ink and quiet typography.

There are no infinite-scroll dopamine loops. No notifications begging you back. No price-anchored urgency. The cartography is hand-toned. The thumbnails sit like polaroids on cream card stock. The search bar is glass. The wordmark is letterpress.

The whole thing is built to make the physical visit feel inevitable — like the app was already on the way to the mall the moment you opened it.
