Review page refresh — v1.2

After the vendor picks a photo from the Add sheet, they land here. The photograph renders exactly like shoppers will see it on the Find Detail page — no crop, same post-it, same everything. The title/caption/price fields sit below.

What you're deciding

  • The photo fix. Three frames below show the same page with three different photos — a portrait phone shot, a near-square shot, and a landscape shot. In every case the full photo is visible. Paper fills the rest. This is what shoppers will see. What you enter here = what goes live.
  • The post-it is on preview. I decided to keep it. The photo-plus-post-it is the signature Treehouse look; hiding it on preview would mean you can't see your full find.
  • Fields unchanged in spirit, cleaner in look. Title, Caption, Price — same order as today, but now they match the rest of the app's input style instead of the legacy v0.2 chrome.
  • One commit button at the bottom: "Save to Shelf." Same green as everywhere else in the app.
  • The Edit/Done pill pattern on each field retires. Today you have to tap "Edit" on each field before typing — that's friction. In v1.2 the fields are just always editable.

The photo fix (in plain English)

Today's preview crops your photo to fit the screen. On Find Detail it uncrops. Different pictures. Bad. In v1.2 the preview IS the Find Detail page's photograph — same shape, same post-it, same border. When your phone's photo is taller than the frame, paper fills the sides. When it's wider, paper fills above and below. Nothing is ever cut off.

What stayed the same

Your three fields: Title, Caption, Price, in that order. Save button at the bottom. AI still writes a caption for you when the page loads (unchanged — that wasn't broken). Your posting identity (ZenForged Finds · Booth 369 · America's Antique Mall) shows right below the photo so you always know which shelf this is going to.

What changed

Georgia serif retires, replaced by the same IM Fell English used everywhere else. Legacy beige background replaces with your paperCream. Form fields match the rest of the app instead of their own v0.2 chrome. The Edit/Done pill pattern on each field retires — just type.

Three quick calls for you after you see it on your phone

  1. Does the photo-with-post-it on the preview feel right? Or would it be less busy to hide the post-it here and only show it on the actual Find Detail page? (My call was: keep it, because this IS the shopper view. But it's a legit call the other way.)
  2. The page title "Review your find" + subhead "Here's how shoppers will see it." Good? Too wordy? Something else?
  3. The save button copy: "Save to Shelf." Current wording. Keep it? "Post to Shelf"? "Publish"? "Add to Shelf" to match what they just tapped on the previous screen?
