Card container exploration — 3 directions × 2 aspect treatments

Mocked against Home. Same 4 placeholder photos across all frames so the "unification against random imagery" effect is visible. Compare horizontally to see direction differences; compare vertically to see how each direction handles aspect variation.

The three directions

  • A. Polaroid evolved — Warm-cream paper card (#faf2e0, slightly off-white). Generous bottom mat for a handwritten Caveat caption. Closest to today's tile, but richer paper feel. Single material layer. Reads as "snapshot."
  • B. Window mat print — Postit cream card (#fbf3df) with a 14px mat border on all four sides. Photo recessed with subtle inset shadow. Lora-italic caption sits on the mat below the photo. Single material with deep border. Reads as "matted print."
  • C. Postage stamp on paper — Two-layer card: postit cream paper (tilted -1°) carries a smaller white stamp (tilted +0.6°) with dashed perforation border. Postmark area below the stamp shows date + cancellation arc. Two material layers. Reads as "collected/archived."

The aspect axis: top row crops every photo to 1:1 (current behavior — fixed crop is the unifier). Bottom row lets each photo show its natural aspect (3:4, 1:1, 4:5, 4:3) inside the same container — testing whether the container can absorb varying photo shapes without losing visual cohesion.
