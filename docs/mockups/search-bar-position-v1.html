The contract: As the user scrolls the feed, the masthead + SearchBar + MallStrip all stay pinned to the top. Feed content scrolls underneath them, hidden by the chrome stack's opaque v2.bg.main backgrounds.

Why this fixes the friction: in the current state, SearchBar scrolls away after the first viewport so the user can't refine their search without scrolling all the way back up. Sticky SearchBar means search is always one tap away regardless of feed scroll depth — matches the always-visible location identity on the strip.

Tradeoff: Fixed chrome stack reduces feed-visible area to ~480px on a 667px-tall iPhone SE (~72% of viewport). On iPhone 12+ (~844px) that's ~78% — fine. The ~70% floor is roughly the same as a typical mobile-web header+nav app, so this isn't unusual chrome-load for the pattern.

Reading order: Top-to-bottom now reads as "where am I (brand) → what am I looking for (search) → where am I shopping (location strip) → finds." The "what am I looking for" question precedes the location qualifier — which matches the digital-to-physical thesis sharper than the inverse: shoppers know what they want, the location is the bridge to the real-world place that has it.
