Vendor CTA — Home page footer

Three card directions to replace the current centered hairline + pill button at the bottom of the feed. Each shown in feed-context so the eye lands on it the way it would on iPhone.

What's being decided

  • Today: centered hairline · italic muted line · green pill button. Low-key, easy to miss. ([app/page.tsx:963-1002](app/page.tsx:963))
  • Goal: something polished and intentional — the last thing a non-vendor visitor sees before BottomNav, and worth paying attention to.
  • Constraint: still lives at the bottom of the feed, sits in warm parchment, uses forest green and IM Fell. Not a banner that interrupts the feed.
  • Out of scope: moving the CTA elsewhere on the page, changing the destination route (/vendor-request), changing what happens after tap.

Three variants below

  • α — Sketch literal: exactly your reference. Icon-row card with chevron + green pill button below. Two affordances (the row tap and the button) both go to the same place.
  • β — Single-tap card: the row IS the button. No separate green pill below. Green chevron-pill on the right is the visible affordance. Cleaner, removes redundancy.
  • γ — Editorial banner: bigger card, IM Fell display headline, storefront icon as a decorative watermark behind the text, integrated button. Treats the CTA as a small editorial moment, not a list row.

What I'd pick — and why

  • β (single-tap card) is my pick. It honors your sketch (icon-row with copy + affordance on the right) but removes the visual stutter of having the same action twice. The IM Fell italic headline gives it brand voice the current line doesn't have. Tap target is the whole card, which is more forgiving than a 120px-wide button on iPhone.
  • γ is the strongest "polished and intentional" read but carries the most visual weight — borderline banner-feel. Worth picking if you want this to read as a moment, not a footer.
  • α is the safest because it matches your sketch one-to-one. Pick this if you want belt-and-suspenders affordance (chevron AND button).
