Share Sheet — QR Code Design

All three options use the same QR data. The question is how the container feels — how branded, how much presence, how much it reads as "Treehouse."

What are we deciding?

A — Vintage label card: Dark header bar with "Treehouse Finds" + arrow, postit body with booth name + mall name alongside the QR, URL footer strip. Feels like a physical hang-tag or shipping label from the shop — very on-brand with the antique market aesthetic.

B — Logo overlay: QR code with the Treehouse logo centered over it (the logo sits on a postit background cutout so it doesn't break scanning). Clean, minimal, modern. The logo makes it immediately recognizable as a Treehouse thing without extra decoration.

C — Green brand wash: QR code on a green-tinted background matching the app's active/brand color. More visually assertive — the green "claims" the QR as clearly belonging to Treehouse. Works if you want the QR to have strong presence in the sheet.
