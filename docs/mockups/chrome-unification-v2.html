Single mental model (chip-overlay refinement)

  • Masthead = hero photo cropped to 130px strip + logo at vertical center (y≈65). Same visual chrome across every surface (except /admin).
  • Mall chip on Home overlays the masthead bottom edge. Chip sticky-pins at top:100 with z-index ABOVE the hero strip. Chip's top edge sits 30px ABOVE the masthead's bottom edge (y=130), so the chip "trims" the bottom of the photo by occluding it. From the user's eye: photo visible y=0 to y=100 (where chip top edge cuts it), photo extends BEHIND chip from y=100 to y=130. Logo at y=65 sits comfortably in the upper portion of the visible photo, never abutting the chip.
  • Explore is the only surface where the hero is bigger than the strip at rest. Logo sticks at viewport-y ≈ masthead vcenter (y≈65). Hero sticky-collapses around it. Chip rises with scroll and locks into its overlay position at y=100.
  • Saved + /find + /shelf + /me + others render the 130px strip from rest. No animation, no full hero, no chip overlay — the full 130px photo strip is visible since no chip occludes it. Just strip + logo + page content below.
  • Affordances (Profile / Back / Share / Flag bubbles) sit OVER the hero strip with the warm-cream backdrop-filter: blur bubble bg for contrast on a photographic surface (light bg + dark glyph). Floating Profile + Back overlays on (tabs)/ stay as session 166 dial 9 geometry. In-masthead slots on /find + /shelf use the same bubble vocabulary.

Risks / open implementation questions

  • iOS Safari sticky-overlap + z-index reliability: Home has THREE sticky elements (hero + logo + chip) converging at different y values with explicit z-index ordering (chip z=38 over hero z=30). iOS Safari has known quirks with sticky elements that have explicit z-index — sometimes the chip's "above" status gets dropped during fast scroll. Testbed validation needed via /sticky-test smoke route per feedback_testbed_first_for_ai_unknowns before consumer wiring
  • Logo's sticky journey: logo is a SIBLING of hero at the layout level (not a child), so it CSS-sticky-pins to viewport top regardless of hero's scroll state. Math: position:sticky;top:calc(max(14px,safe-area)+51px) (= masthead vertical center at 130px strip). Logo's at-rest position derived from its natural document flow position (above hero in source order with a negative-margin offset) so it APPEARS centered in the full 33vh hero at scroll=0, then sticky-engages at threshold
  • Bubble contrast on hero strip: BG.png has dark woodgrain center + lighter edges. Logo over center reads fine (transparent PNG with warm-brown ink). Bubbles in slot positions (left + right) sit over edges of the photo which may be brighter — light-bubble-bg + dark-glyph should still read. iPhone QA will tell. The 130px strip gives more vertical room for the affordances too — they're now sitting in a taller chrome with breathing room above + below the 44px buttons
  • Bottom-fade target color varies per surface: Home + Saved use v2.bg.tabs (#E6DECF) — the masthead's bottom edge bleeds INTO the (tabs)/ surface bg. /find + /shelf + /me use v2.bg.main (#F7F3EB) — slightly different cream. The <HeroMastheadStrip> primitive needs a fadeTarget prop. NOTE: on Home, the strip's bottom 30px is OCCLUDED by chip anyway, so the fade target there is moot (chip's solid bg covers it). The fade matters most on non-Home surfaces where the full 130px is visible
  • Status-bar text color across surfaces: hero photo bg means light status-bar text. Across the whole app. Easy to set globally via theme-color meta + apple-mobile-web-app-status-bar-style. Was previously cream-bg + dark text — now needs flip
  • Chip's drop-shadow direction is up, not down: normal cards cast shadows downward. The chip "scroll over the top of the hero" effect wants a shadow that suggests the chip is layered ABOVE the photo — so the shadow casts UPWARD onto the photo it's covering. CSS: box-shadow: 0 -2px 8px rgba(42,26,10,0.10);. Subtle but reinforces the "layered over" reading
