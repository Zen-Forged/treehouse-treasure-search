Feed timestamp — placement options

Six variants — three on-image, three below-image — for the relative timestamp on each masonry tile.

What this mockup is asking you to judge

The feed is currently photograph-only — every tile is just the image with the heart bubble top-right. Adding a relative timestamp introduces new chrome. On-image keeps the masonry rhythm tight (no text below). Below-image reads more clearly but adds a meta line under every tile.

  • A — Top-left frosted pill (mirrors heart's chrome, visual pair)
  • B — Bottom-left frosted pill (less competition with heart)
  • C — Bottom-left "darkroom date stamp" — italic IM Fell on photo, no chip
  • D — Below tile, right-aligned, small system muted
  • E — Below tile, italic IM Fell editorial line ("Posted 2d ago")
  • F — Below tile, centered tiny with em-dashes
Decisions to make:
  1. Placement — on-image (A/B/C) or below-image (D/E/F)?
  2. Voice — system-ui terse ("2d ago") or editorial ("Posted 2d ago")?
  3. Visibility on photo — frosted pill (A/B) or just text on the photo (C)?
1 On the image Tight masonry rhythm preserved. Risk: timestamps fight with photo content.
