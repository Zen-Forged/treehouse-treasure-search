Onboarding flow refresh — v1.2

Full onboarding sweep. Name splits into First/Last, booth name becomes its own field, booth photo required as commitment + verification gesture, duplicate requests handled gracefully, emails rebuilt on the v1.1l paper surface using Georgia throughout (IM Fell retired from email).

What this mockup is committing to

  • New Name split + booth name. /vendor-request captures first_name, last_name, and a new optional booth_name field. If booth name is blank at approval, vendors.display_name defaults to first_name + " " + last_name. Schema: three new columns on vendor_requests, one new column on vendors (already exists as display_name). Old vendor_requests.name column stays populated for backwards compat during rollout.
  • Sprint 5 Post-approval booth-name edit deferred. Primary problem (vendor inherits their own name as booth name with no choice) is solved by giving them the field at request time. Edit-after-approval gets its own mini-mockup + Sprint 5 ticket.
  • New Booth photo required (Model B). One field, warm framing: "Show us your booth — a photo of your sign, your name tag, or anything with your booth number on it." Proves tenancy, builds commitment (~3 min of real effort), fills the empty-shelf problem on day one.
  • New Email dedup. Server-side pre-check in POST /api/vendor-request. Pending-match → warm in-place "We already have you" state (no duplicate insert, no second email). Approved-match → same treatment, different copy nudging to sign in. Partial unique index ON (email) WHERE status='pending' as DB safety net.
  • Design Emails on v1.1l paper-as-surface, all Georgia. Old templates used a white card on #f0ede6 (v0.2 chrome). New templates drop the card. Paper #e8ddc7 is the container. IM Fell retired from all three email templates — brand lockup, eyebrows, and signatures now all Georgia. Safest across every mail client, zero external font load, fastest render.
  • Sprint 5 Typography reassessment logged. IM Fell's readability tax (especially at 13px italic labels) gets its own sprint. Candidates to evaluate: EB Garamond, Lora, Fraunces, or all-Georgia. The PWA form in this mockup still shows IM Fell because this session isn't the place to resolve it — but it's on the list.
  • PWA Email #2 has no clickable in-app link. Old CTA opened /login?redirect=/setup in a Safari tab, breaking the installed PWA session. New copy: "Open Treehouse on your phone, tap Sign In, and enter this email:" with the email echoed in a copyable pill.

Questions this mockup is asking you to judge

  • /vendor-request form (A1/A1b): The split name + booth name fields feel right in order? Booth-name helper copy ("Leave blank to use your name") clear?
  • Booth photo field: Does "Show us your booth" with the wide-shot hint feel warm rather than gatekeeping?
  • Already-pending state (A2): Clock glyph + "We already have you" — reads warmly, doesn't feel like an error?
  • Emails on all-Georgia (B1–B3): Brand lockup at Georgia 26px bold holds enough presence without IM Fell? Or does it feel too plain now?
