Three still-frame snapshots of the photograph in flight from a feed tile to /find/[id]. Same animation runs in reverse on back. Three surfaces in scope: feed → /find/[id], /shelves → /shelf/[slug], /flagged → /find/[id]. BoothPage shelf rail tiles pulled to v2.

Decisions to lock at this stage

#QuestionRecommendation
D7 Easing curve for the photograph in flight cubic-bezier(0.32, 0.72, 0, 1) — the iOS-native “spring-out” curve. Matches FB Marketplace’s feel: slightly overshooting, decelerating into final position. Already the curve app/page.tsx MasonryTile uses (intentional polish on the hero feed grid).
D8 Duration 340ms forward, 300ms back. Forward needs the extra 40ms for the photo to read as “arriving”; back is faster to reward the back-tap as a quick exit. Within the typical iOS-native range (300–400ms).
D9 Surrounding chrome on the source page during flight Faded to ~18% opacity, 220ms ease-out. Other tiles + filter chips fade as the photograph leaves; masthead persists at full opacity (per D3). Page background stays paper.
D10 Z-index of flying photo vs. masthead Below masthead (z-index 20 vs. masthead 40). The photo passes under the masthead’s blur band — feels like FB Marketplace, keeps masthead as a stable reference point.
D11 Post-it crossfade timing Tile post-it fades out 0–160ms, detail post-it fades in 200–360ms. Small overlap of empty (40ms with no post-it visible) reads as a clean handoff. Tile post-it does NOT travel (per D1).
D12 Detail-page secondary content (title, caption, cartographic block) Stagger in after photo arrives — title at 280ms (delay 40ms after photo lands), caption at 340ms, divider + cartographic at 400ms. Same MOTION_CARD_DURATION + MOTION_STAGGER tokens added in session 76. Reuses the existing entrance pattern.
D13 Performance fallback framer-motion <MotionConfig reducedMotion="user"> at the app shell. When OS reports reduced-motion, transitions fall back to instant nav (matches existing framer-motion call sites that respect reduced-motion natively).
D14 Image source for the layoutId target Same image_url on both ends; aspect ratio shifts during flight (3/4 tile → 4/5 detail). object-fit: cover handles the morph; no separate image variants.
