Save glyph — replace the heart

The heart reads as a commerce/social "like." Treehouse Finds isn't a buy-online platform — saving a find is closer to marking it on your find map. Five flag/marker candidates compared below.

What this mockup is asking you to judge

Pick one glyph to replace the heart everywhere it currently signals "save." Each variant shown unsaved (cream outline) and saved (filled green) in the feed-tile bubble at production size (36px bubble, 18px glyph), plus a 32px scale-up below for shape clarity.

  • A — Triangular flag (right) — pole + right-pointing pennant, claim-the-spot energy
  • B — Rectangular flag — pole + cloth, classic survey marker
  • C — Bookmark ribbon — V-bottom tag, "saved for later" metaphor without commerce flavor
  • D — Drop pin (alt to mall pin) — round top + sharp tip, distinct from the existing PinGlyph used for malls
  • E — Pushpin / thumbtack — physical "pin to my map" gesture
System impact: The chosen glyph swaps in 5 places — feed tile bubble, Find Detail photo bubble, Find Map (/flagged) tile, BottomNav "saved" tab icon, and the empty-state glyph on /shelf/[slug]. The heart's color treatment (cream-outlined unsaved → green-filled saved) carries across.
Decisions to make:
  1. Glyph — A / B / C / D / E?
  2. Saved-state fill — keep current green-fill (matches Find Detail), or different (e.g. green outline only, no fill)?
  3. BottomNav tab label — currently "Your Finds." Stays the same, or rethink alongside the glyph swap?
