Two button-font treatments

  • Treatment 1 — Sans semibold, normal case: system sans · 14px · weight 600 · "Request Digital Booth". Reads modern and action-oriented. Friendly, app-native register.
  • Treatment 2 — Sans medium, tracked uppercase: system sans · 11px · weight 600 · letter-spacing 0.14em · "REQUEST DIGITAL BOOTH". Reads editorial and retail-store. Premium register — pairs with the IM Fell italic headline as a "luxury-shop" combo.

What I'd pick — and why

  • Treatment 1 (sans semibold) is my pick. The card already has two display-register elements pulling visual weight (the IM Fell italic headline and the FOR VENDORS eyebrow). A third tracked-uppercase element on the button starts to feel like every word wants to be read. Treatment 1 lets the button just be a button — clear action, no register competition with the headline. Editorial feel comes from the headline; the button does the work.
  • Treatment 2 is the right pick if the dual-placement (empty-state when a mall has no items) wants to feel more like a department-store window. More premium, more retail-y, but pulls slightly more attention.
