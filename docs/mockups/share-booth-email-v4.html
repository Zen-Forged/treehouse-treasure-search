Window email — v4 simplified (button-forward, masthead retired)

Trim toward a single-CTA email. The top is noticeably lighter; the button is elevated right under the banner, not buried at the end. The 6-tile grid stays as a supporting preview but loses its eyebrow. The goal is a well-designed email that gets people to open the link to see more finds.

What changes in v4

1. Top strip — drastically trimmed

  • Cut The "TREEHOUSE FINDS" SMALL masthead + "Embrace the Search. Treasure the Find." tagline. Sender is identified by the mail envelope ("Treehouse Finds" in the From row); re-identifying in the body was redundant.
  • Cut The two-line opener — "You've received a personal invite." + "Step inside a curated booth from" eyebrow.
  • New One italic line: "You've received a personal invite to explore" — the sentence flows directly into the vendor name as its object.
  • New Vendor name hero stays IM Fell, bumps from 32px → 34px since it's now the undisputed top visual (no masthead / eyebrow competing for weight).

2. Button elevated + renamed

  • Move The green pill CTA moves from the bottom of the email to directly under the banner + info bar. It's the main action — it should be in context.
  • New Button copy: "Explore the booth" (was: "Open in Treehouse Finds"). Tighter, three words, verb-first.
  • Cut "Explore the rest of the booth" italic eyebrow.
  • Cut "More treasures waiting to be discovered." sub-copy.
  • Cut The border-top hairline that separated the old closer block from the Window grid. No longer needed — tiles flow directly from the button.

3. Window grid — tiles stay, eyebrow goes

  • Cut "THE WINDOW" italic-uppercase eyebrow above the tiles.
  • New Grid stands naked under the button — 6 tiles with their captions. Self-explanatory preview of what's inside the booth.

4. What stays unchanged

  • Banner + attached 2-cell info bar (Variant A from v3 — BOOTH numeral + mall name + dotted-underline address)
  • The 6-tile 3×2 grid with system-ui captions
  • Green pill CTA styling (just shorter copy)
  • Footer hairline + tiny "You're receiving this because…" text
  • IM Fell Google Fonts <link> (still used by the vendor name + mall name)
  • Subject line: "A Window into {vendor}"
  • Preheader: "A personal invite to a curated booth."
  • senderMode remains server-side for rate-limit buckets; template still ignores it

Questions for your call

  • Q1 Variant A (pill) or Variant B (full-width block)? Two button shapes below. Pill = elegant, smaller tap target. Full-width = maximum clickability on mobile, feels more like a modern app CTA.
  • Q2 Preheader — keep "A personal invite to a curated booth." or align it with the new body copy? E.g. "A personal invite to explore {vendor}." Matches the body's first line. Downside: includes a variable, slightly less universal.
  • Q3 Subject line — keep "A Window into {vendor}" or align with new voice? E.g. "A personal invite to {vendor}." or "Step into {vendor}." "Window" is being retired from body copy; should it retire from subject too? Or is the subject-line brand more entrenched?
